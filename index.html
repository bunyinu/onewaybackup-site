What OneWay does

OneWay keeps a protected copy of your photos and videos that your phone’s deletions can’t reach. If you delete something by accident or wipe your library to free space, your memories stay safe, and you can restore any of them in one tap.

Frequently asked

Where are my photos stored?

They stay inside your own Apple account — they never pass through us, we run no servers, and we can’t see them.

Does it use my storage?

Yes — OneWay keeps a separate, protected copy, so it uses some of your own storage. That second copy is exactly what lets your photos survive deletion.

What does “Recovered from deletion” mean?

When you delete a backed-up photo from the Photos app, OneWay keeps its copy and lists it under Recovered, so you can put it back anytime — with no duplicates.

If I delete my account, do I lose my photos?

No. Deleting your account (Settings → Account → Delete Account) removes your account and the app’s local data from your device. Your backed-up photos are not deleted — you can sign back in to see them anytime.

Does OneWay read my other files or track me?

No. OneWay only reads your photo library (with your permission) to back it up. It has no access to your documents, messages, or other apps’ data, and uses no trackers or advertising.
